Breaking Language Barriers with LearnEase

Project Overview

LearnEase is a mobile app designed to help newcomers to Canada overcome language barriers through real-world scenarios, real-time translation, and interactive learning. Unlike traditional language apps, it focuses on practical, everyday experiences like doctor visits and job applications.

Context

My group and I designed LearnEase to help newcomers to Canada overcome language barriers in everyday life. Many struggle with tasks like visiting a doctor, job searching, or using public transit due to limited English proficiency. LearnEase takes a practical approach by using scenario-based learning, real-time translation, and interactive tools to build confidence and make communication easier.

My Role

In the LearnEase project, I focused on ideation, research, and content creation, making sure our solution was practical and user-friendly. I worked closely with my team to shape the project’s direction, basing our design decisions on real user needs and challenges.

User Research

Conducted research on language barriers, cultural adaptation, and existing solutions to inform our design approach.

Ideation & Concept Development

Contributed to brainstorming sessions to define the app’s purpose, features, and user experience.

Content Strategy & Writing

Developed clear and engaging text for the app interface, scenario-based learning modules, and supporting materials.

Our Research Approach

To understand the real challenges immigrants face, we conducted user interviews, secondary research, and case studies on language acquisition and cultural adaptation. These insights shaped our design decisions, ensuring the app provides practical, real-world support.

Key Features

Scenario-Based Learning

Practice real-life situations like doctor visits and job interviews.

Real-Time Translation

Instantly translate conversations for smoother communication.

Personalized Experience

Tailored learning paths based on user needs.

Gamified Learning

Fun, interactive ways to build language skills.

Our Application

LearnEase was an opportunity to explore how user-centered design and research-driven insights can create meaningful digital experiences. While this was primarily a class project focused on research and problem-solving, we put thought into designing a solution that is both practical and engaging. If given more time, this app would be fully prototyped for further user testing and iteration, allowing us to refine the experience and expand its impact.

Design Process

a user-centred design approach, moving through several phases:

  1. Research & Ideation – Understanding the problem and brainstorming potential solutions.
  2. Wireframing & Prototyping – Creating low-fidelity wireframes to map out user flows.
  3. User Testing & Iteration – Refining the experience based on feedback.
  4. Final Design & Implementation – Developing a structured, engaging, and accessible interface.
